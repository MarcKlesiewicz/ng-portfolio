As part of my education as a computer science AP graduate, I collaborated with the app agency LittleGiants to develop a product for one of their clients – Monto.


Monto is a danish rental platform that allows private individuals and businesses to rent out items within their local area. The platform enables owners to earn money on everything from tools, clothing, car-trailers, speakers and board games. It can best be described as a kind of "Airbnb" for everyday items.


Notable features include:

  • Rental item management: CRUD operations (Create, Read, Update, Delete), including image upload for each item.

  • Complex calendar management: Scheduling rental periods and handling bulk discounts.

  • Filtering of rental items: Search based on keywords, main and subcategories, as well as geographic location with boundaries.

  • Rental agreement management: A structured process for creating and maintaining rental agreements.

  • 1-to-1 live chat: A direct communication channel between the renter and the owner.

  • Review and rating system: Users can leave reviews and ratings to help others choose reliable owners and products.

  • Admin: Management of main and subcategories, platform statistics/key metrics, reporting.



My role in the project was to implement the frontend of the application using Flutter. Although it was my first experience with cross-platform development, I had the opportunity to develop both large and small parts of Monto.


The project was an invaluable learning experience where I gained skills in component-based programming, software architecture, best practices for mobile UI design concepts, and real project management.

Working with Flutter inspired me to continue exploring and specializing in app development, and about a year later, I had the opportunity to implement the web version of Monto – where, for a period, I took on the role of lead developer.
